Olympics (2014)

The picture in 2014 now reflects the turn-around year of the Olympics, where the United States emerged as an incredibly robust and competitive player. The United States secured 145 gold medals, On the contrary, world number one till the last Olympics Canada secured only one. Although old countries maintained their positions in the league, few more countries emerged and became part of the race.

In 2014 Olympics there were total 632 gold medals.

Olympics (2016)

In 2016, While the United States again maintained the first position and secured astonishing 139 gold medals, significant progress was seen among the growth of other countries. Great Britain rose up as the world’s second and Russia as the world’s third in the league. Canada again could not cope up and remained in a lower position.  

In 2016 Olympics there were total 664 gold medals.
